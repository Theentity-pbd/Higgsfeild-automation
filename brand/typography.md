# PBD London — Typography

## PURPOSE

This file defines the typography system for PBD London across all visual outputs — garments, campaigns, digital content, and brand communications.

Typography must feel:
- luxury
- bold but controlled
- cinematic
- editorial
- modern and premium

---

# TYPOGRAPHY PHILOSOPHY

Type is not decoration.

It is voice made visible.

PBD London typography should communicate the same qualities as the brand itself:
- disciplined
- elevated
- emotionally weighted
- restrained but powerful

Every typographic decision should reinforce the Pressure Builds Diamonds identity.

---

# TYPE PERSONALITY

PBD London type should feel:

- **Confident** — weight and scale that communicates certainty
- **Restrained** — never decorative or trend-driven
- **Cinematic** — type that could exist on a luxury film poster
- **Editorial** — informed by fashion-house visual language
- **Timeless** — never tied to a passing aesthetic moment

Avoid type that feels:
- hype-driven
- streetwear cliché
- generic motivational
- overly playful
- digitally trendy

---

# TYPE HIERARCHY

## Level 01 — Campaign Headline

The primary typographic statement.

Characteristics:
- maximum visual weight
- oversized, commanding scale
- single line or two maximum
- communicates campaign philosophy in compressed form
- reads from a distance

Usage:
- campaign imagery
- key visual posters
- video title cards
- hero product imagery

---

## Level 02 — Subheading / Campaign Sub-Statement

Supporting the headline without competing.

Characteristics:
- reduced weight or scale from headline
- adds context, depth, or emotional layer
- should feel like a secondary thought that strengthens the primary

Usage:
- campaign secondary lines
- supporting graphic elements
- product campaign details

---

## Level 03 — Body / Descriptive Text

Informational layer. Refined and minimal.

Characteristics:
- clean and readable
- premium feel at smaller sizes
- generous line spacing
- never cramped

Usage:
- product descriptions
- campaign detail text
- website and e-commerce copy

---

## Level 04 — Brand Mark / Logo Typography

The PBD London wordmark.

Usage rules:
- consistent placement system across all outputs
- never distorted, stretched, or misused
- respects clear space requirements
- primary placement: lower right, lower centre, or upper left depending on composition

---

# TYPOGRAPHIC BEHAVIOUR

## Preferred Approaches

- **Mixed hierarchy** — contrast between oversized focal type and refined supporting text
- **Layered type interaction** — type sits within the composition, not over it
- **Asymmetric placement** — off-centre positioning consistent with overall design language
- **Controlled scale contrast** — a single dominant type element supported by restrained secondary elements
- **Uppercase for impact** — primary headlines often uppercase for visual power
- **Generous tracking on uppercase** — wide letter-spacing at headline level reinforces luxury positioning

---

## Spacing Rules

- Headlines: generous tracking (letter-spacing), tight to natural leading
- Body text: comfortable leading (line height 1.4–1.6x font size)
- Never compressed text that feels cluttered
- Negative space around type is part of the luxury system

---

# TYPEFACE DIRECTION

## Headline Typefaces

Preferred characteristics:
- high contrast or geometric structure
- confident weight
- clean without being sterile
- works oversized without losing integrity
- has a fashion-editorial precedent

Style references:
- condensed grotesque serifs
- high-contrast display serifs
- premium geometric sans-serifs used with weight

---

## Body Typefaces

Preferred characteristics:
- legible at small sizes
- premium neutral quality
- pairs naturally with headline typeface
- never decorative

Style references:
- clean grotesque sans-serifs
- refined humanist typefaces
- minimal editorial serifs at body weight

---

## Typeface Combinations

Maximum two typeface families per output.

Combination logic:
- one dominant (headline) + one refined (body)
- contrast should come from weight and scale, not from clashing styles
- both must feel like they belong to the same world

---

# GARMENT TYPOGRAPHY

## Placement System

Preferred garment type placement:
- chest: minimal — brand mark or short statement only
- back: primary typographic storytelling location
- sleeve: secondary brand mark or season identifier
- internal: label typography, brand mark

## Scale on Garment

- back graphics: large, commanding, readable at a distance
- chest placement: considered and restrained
- embroidery: proportional to placement area, never oversized

## Embroidery-Specific Rules

Embroidery requires:
- bolder weight than print equivalents (fine details are lost in thread)
- reduced detail in letterforms
- tested at actual production size before approval

---

# WHAT TO AVOID

- excessive typeface variety (more than two families per output)
- cheap font combinations
- distorted or stretched letterforms
- decorative scripts used for primary communication
- trend-driven typefaces with short shelf life
- cluttered text placement
- type that competes with imagery rather than supporting it
- centre-aligned body text at length

---

# FAILURE CONDITIONS

Reject typographic decisions that:
- feel generic or could belong to any brand
- prioritise novelty over brand consistency
- undermine the luxury positioning through low-quality font choices
- create visual noise rather than visual clarity
