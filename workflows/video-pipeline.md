# workflows/video-pipeline.md

# PBD LONDON — SEEDANCE VIDEO PIPELINE

---

# PURPOSE

This workflow defines the complete operational pipeline for generating cinematic PBD London video outputs using Seedance 2.0, Higgsfield automation, Claude Code, and Obsidian logging systems.

The process prioritises:
- consistency
- modularity
- brand integrity
- operational clarity
- scalable automation

---

# PIPELINE OVERVIEW

## WORKFLOW STAGES

1. Brief Intake
2. Campaign Context Analysis
3. Variable Population
4. Prompt Construction
5. Seedance Prompt Validation
6. Higgsfield Submission
7. Output Review
8. Asset Selection
9. Export & Naming
10. Obsidian Logging

---

# STAGE 01 — BRIEF INTAKE

## OBJECTIVE

Receive campaign intent and creative direction.

---

## REQUIRED INPUTS

### Campaign Name
Defines campaign identity.

### Product Focus
Defines featured product or garment.

### Scene Direction
Defines cinematic environment and visual intent.

### Emotional Direction
Defines psychological tone.

### Output Type
Defines:
- teaser
- campaign film
- product visual
- social cut
- cinematic loop

---

## OPTIONAL INPUTS

### Location
### Time Of Day
### Weather
### Accent Colour
### Shot Style
### Subject Type
### Runtime
### Aspect Ratio

---

# STAGE 02 — CAMPAIGN CONTEXT ANALYSIS

## OBJECTIVE

Align brief against existing PBD brand systems.

---

## REQUIRED REFERENCES

Read:
- CREATIVE-DIRECTION.md
- brand-voice.md
- visual-language.md
- operating-rules.md
- seedance-base-prompt.md

---

## VALIDATION CHECKS

Ensure:
- emotional tone matches brand
- visuals align with cinematic direction
- no contradiction with operating rules
- no duplicated campaign concepts
- output maintains premium positioning

---

# STAGE 03 — VARIABLE POPULATION

## OBJECTIVE

Populate all modular prompt variables.

---

## VARIABLE CATEGORIES

### Campaign Variables
- campaign name
- collection
- release phase

### Visual Variables
- location
- atmosphere
- lighting
- motion style
- colour behaviour

### Subject Variables
- wardrobe
- posture
- movement
- emotional energy

### Technical Variables
- aspect ratio
- runtime
- pacing
- output format

---

## POPULATION RULES

Variables must:
- remain concise
- remain cinematic
- avoid filler wording
- preserve emotional clarity

---

# STAGE 04 — PROMPT CONSTRUCTION

## OBJECTIVE

Build final Seedance-ready prompt.

---

## PROCESS

1. Load seedance-base-prompt.md
2. Inject populated variables
3. Preserve original structure
4. Validate cinematic consistency
5. Remove duplicated wording
6. Maintain premium pacing

---

## OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS

Final prompt must:
- feel visually coherent
- maintain emotional realism
- preserve luxury identity
- remain technically readable

---

# STAGE 05 — SEEDANCE PROMPT VALIDATION

## OBJECTIVE

Perform pre-generation quality control.

---

## VALIDATION CHECKLIST

Confirm:
- camera language consistency
- lighting realism
- environment coherence
- subject realism
- pacing alignment
- colour restraint
- emotional consistency

---

## FAILURE CONDITIONS

Reject prompts containing:
- generic AI phrasing
- visual contradiction
- excessive effects
- unrealistic movement
- hype language
- inconsistent tone

---

# STAGE 06 — HIGGSFIELD SUBMISSION

## OBJECTIVE

Submit final validated prompt into Seedance 2.0 through Higgsfield workflow.

---

## SUBMISSION FLOW

1. Open Higgsfield automation workflow
2. Insert validated prompt
3. Confirm output settings
4. Start generation
5. Track generation status

---

## OUTPUT SETTINGS

Preferred:
- cinematic quality
- realistic motion
- atmospheric lighting
- premium texture rendering
- smooth pacing

---

# STAGE 07 — OUTPUT REVIEW

## OBJECTIVE

Review generated assets against PBD standards.

---

## REVIEW CATEGORIES

### Cinematic Quality
### Brand Alignment
### Emotional Weight
### Motion Realism
### Lighting Consistency
### Product Visibility
### Environmental Realism
### Editing Rhythm

---

## REVIEW QUESTIONS

Does the output:
- feel premium?
- feel emotionally controlled?
- preserve cinematic realism?
- communicate pressure visually?
- maintain luxury restraint?

---

# STAGE 08 — ASSET SELECTION

## OBJECTIVE

Approve or reject outputs.

---

## APPROVAL REQUIREMENTS

Approved outputs must:
- align with brand identity
- maintain emotional discipline
- preserve visual realism
- feel editorial and cinematic
- avoid generic AI aesthetics

---

## REJECTION CONDITIONS

Reject outputs containing:
- unstable motion
- poor anatomy
- unrealistic lighting
- exaggerated effects
- weak emotional direction
- low production feel

---

# STAGE 09 — EXPORT & NAMING

## OBJECTIVE

Export approved assets using standardised naming conventions.

---

## FILE NAMING STRUCTURE

Format:
YYYYMMDD-campaign-scene-version

Example:
20260522-pressure-season-rooftop-v01

---

## EXPORT REQUIREMENTS

Store:
- final renders
- selected stills
- prompt versions
- revision notes

---

## REQUIRED LOG FIELDS

### Generation Date
