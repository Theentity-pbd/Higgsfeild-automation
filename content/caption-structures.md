# PBD London — Caption Structures

## PURPOSE

This file defines the structural frameworks for PBD London captions across platforms.

For the operational prompt template with variables, see `prompts/caption-prompt.md`.
This file defines the structural logic behind how captions are built.

---

# CAPTION PHILOSOPHY

A caption is not a description.

It is an extension of the visual.

PBD London captions should feel like they exist in the same world as the image or video they accompany — same atmosphere, same emotional weight, same restraint.

The caption should deepen what the visual communicates, not explain it.

---

# STRUCTURAL PRINCIPLES

## Economy of Language

Every word must earn its place.

If a word can be removed without losing meaning or atmosphere, remove it.

Filler words communicate low confidence.
Restraint communicates premium positioning.

---

## Rhythm Before Rules

Read every caption aloud before publishing.

It must have:
- natural rhythm
- controlled pacing
- emotional momentum from first to last line

If it does not read with rhythm, it is not finished.

---

## Emotional Progression

Every caption should move through an emotional arc:

Open → Deepen → Close

The reader should end in a different emotional state than they began.

---

# CAPTION ANATOMY

## Opening Line

The single most important line.

Requirements:
- creates emotional tension immediately
- implies pressure, transformation, or discipline
- does not over-explain
- works as a standalone statement

See `storytelling-hooks.md` for hook frameworks.

---

## Body (when used)

The middle section deepens the atmosphere.

Requirements:
- reinforces the opening without repeating it
- adds one layer of meaning
- maintains restraint
- never over-writes

Body should be:
- 1–3 lines maximum for most platforms
- used only when it adds genuine depth
- absent when the opening line is complete on its own

---

## Closing Line

The emotional residue.

Requirements:
- leaves the reader with something to carry
- feels memorable without trying to be
- subtle brand reinforcement
- understated

The closing line should make the reader pause, not click away immediately.

---

## CTA (when used)

Restrained, minimal, premium.

Approved approaches:
- "discover the collection"
- "available now"
- "entering soon"
- "explore further"
- "built under pressure"
- "campaign live"

Never:
- BUY NOW
- LIMITED TIME ONLY
- DON'T MISS OUT
- aggressive conversion language
- urgency tactics

# PLATFORM-SPECIFIC STRUCTURES

## Instagram

Structure:
```
[Opening line — emotional tension]

[Body — 1–3 lines deepening atmosphere]

[Closing line — emotional residue]

[CTA — if applicable, restrained]

[Hashtags — 5–10, separated from copy]
```

Tone: cinematic, premium editorial, visually connected to the image.

Length: short to medium. Never a wall of text.

---

## TikTok

Structure:
```
[Opening hook — maximum 1 line]
[Tension or philosophy — 1 line]
```

Maximum: 2–3 lines visible before cut-off.

Hashtags: 3–5 maximum. Placed after copy.

Tone: sharp, emotionally immediate, culturally aware.

---

## X

Structure:
```
[Single idea — complete in one statement]
```

If continuation is needed:
```
[Line 1 — statement]
[Line 2 — depth or contrast]
```

Maximum: 2 lines. Singular idea. No hashtags required.

Tone: direct, disciplined, quotable, psychologically clean.

---

## Product Descriptions (Website)

Structure:
```
[Single atmospheric line — sets emotional context]

[Product description — minimal, focused on material and construction]

[Brand line — optional, restrained]
```

Tone: minimal, clean, premium, emotionally intelligent. Clarity-focused.

No superlatives. No cheap hype.

---

## Campaign Headlines

Structure:
```
[Single statement — 3–7 words maximum]
```

Requirements:
- communicates the core campaign philosophy
- works visually large on campaign imagery
- sounds like PBD London, not a generic brand

Examples of approach (not templates):
- Short noun/verb statements
- Transformation language
- Pressure philosophy compressed to its minimum

---

# LINE BREAK USAGE

Line breaks are rhythmic tools, not formatting rules.

Use line breaks to:
- control pacing
- create pause
- separate emotional beats
- allow each line to land before the next arrives

Do not use line breaks to:
- make short content appear longer
- force a structure that does not exist naturally
- pad captions with visual space

---

# WHAT TO AVOID

- Generic motivational phrasing
- Influencer language and slang
- Emoji as emotional substitutes
- Over-explanation of the visual
- Brand name overuse within the caption
- Captions that could belong to any brand
- Algorithm-bait wording
- Forced rhyme or poetic structure that feels artificial

---

# FAILURE CONDITIONS

Reject captions that:
- explain the visual rather than extend it
- feel disconnected from the image or video atmosphere
- use language that breaks brand voice
- prioritise length over impact
- sound like generic motivational content
