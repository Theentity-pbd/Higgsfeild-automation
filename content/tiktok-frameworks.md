# PBD London — TikTok Frameworks

## PURPOSE

This file defines the content frameworks, video structures, and strategic behaviour for PBD London on TikTok.

TikTok content must feel:
- emotionally immediate
- psychologically engaging
- culturally fluid
- premium without being alienating
- cinematic in feel, native in format

---

# PLATFORM PHILOSOPHY

TikTok is a psychological environment, not a showcase.

The first second determines everything.

PBD London on TikTok should feel like:
- stumbling across something real
- a moment of emotional resonance
- calm confidence in a loud environment
- premium restraint that stands out through stillness

Never chase the algorithm.
Build content the algorithm has to recognise.

---

# CONTENT PILLARS

## 01 — Cinematic Brand Moments

Short cinematic clips that feel like excerpts from a larger film.

Characteristics:
- slow motion or restrained movement
- atmospheric audio
- no direct selling
- brand identity through visual language alone

Length: 7–20 seconds

---

## 02 — Pressure Philosophy

Short-form content communicating the PBD mindset.

Characteristics:
- single idea per video
- visually grounded
- voiceover or caption-driven
- emotionally weighted

Length: 15–30 seconds

---

## 03 — Product in Context

Product shown within cinematic environments, not staged.

Characteristics:
- garment featured naturally within a scene
- no static product shots
- movement, texture, and environment reveal the product
- never feels like an advertisement

Length: 10–25 seconds

---

## 04 — Campaign Teasers

Atmospheric previews of upcoming campaigns or drops.

Characteristics:
- intentionally incomplete
- creates curiosity without explanation
- visual tension over information
- end on unanswered atmosphere

Length: 5–15 seconds

---

## 05 — Behind the Process

Content showing creative discipline, not lifestyle.

Characteristics:
- focused on craft, not personality
- quiet and intentional
- communicates the seriousness behind the brand
- never casual or unplanned in feel

Length: 20–45 seconds

---

# VIDEO STRUCTURE FRAMEWORKS

## Framework A — Atmospheric Pull

Used for: cinematic brand moments, campaign teasers

Structure:
1. Strong visual or audio hook (0–2s)
2. Atmosphere establishes (2–8s)
3. Single emotional beat lands (8–15s)
4. Hold or cut to black (15–20s)

No explanation required.
No CTA required.
Let the visual communicate.

---

## Framework B — Tension and Release

Used for: pressure philosophy content, product reveals

Structure:
1. Statement or visual creates tension (0–3s)
2. Tension deepens — visuals or copy layer in (3–10s)
3. Release moment — product, quote, or silence (10–18s)
4. Restrained close (18–25s)

---

## Framework C — Single Idea

Used for: philosophy content, quotable moments

Structure:
1. Single line or image hits immediately (0–2s)
2. One supporting visual or thought (2–8s)
3. End — do not over-explain (8–15s)

Short, weighted, done.

---

## Framework D — Journey Cut

Used for: product in context, process content

Structure:
1. Subject in motion through environment (0–5s)
2. Detail reveal — garment, texture, focus (5–12s)
3. Return to movement or environmental wide (12–20s)
4. Atmospheric close (20–25s)

---

# HOOK PRINCIPLES

The first 1–2 seconds must:
- create immediate emotional tension
- imply something worth staying for
- feel premium and intentional
- never beg for attention

Approved hook approaches:
- visual contrast (stillness in movement, subject in empty space)
- a single powerful line delivered without performance
- unexpected silence against environmental sound
- a close-up detail that creates curiosity

Disallowed hook approaches:
- "wait for it" energy
- direct address to the camera
- excessive text overlays
- jump scares or shock tactics
- trend-chasing formats

---

# CAPTION BEHAVIOUR ON TIKTOK

Short form only.

Structure:
- fast emotional hook (first line)
- tension or philosophy statement
- no hashtag overload

Maximum: 2–3 lines visible before cut-off.

Hashtags: 3–5 maximum, brand and fashion relevant only.

---

# AUDIO STRATEGY

Audio should:
- support atmosphere, not define it
- feel like a discovery, not a trend sound
- use ambient and cinematic audio when possible
- use music that reinforces emotional weight over viral potential

Avoid:
- trending audio used because it is trending
- music that undermines the visual weight
- audio that feels mismatched with the PBD aesthetic

---

# PERFORMANCE METRICS PHILOSOPHY

Do not optimise for:
- views at the cost of brand integrity
- viral formats that dilute identity
- comment bait
- trend participation that weakens positioning

Optimise for:
- saves and shares over likes
- profile visits from the right audience
- consistent retention from aligned viewers
- community growth around pressure and elevation identity

---

# FAILURE CONDITIONS

Reject TikTok content that:
- feels like it is trying to go viral
- looks like every other streetwear brand
- chases trends at the cost of brand identity
- lacks emotional intent
- feels produced for the platform rather than the brand
