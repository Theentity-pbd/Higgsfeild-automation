# PBD London — Motion Direction

## PURPOSE

This file defines subject movement, physical performance, and pacing behaviour for all PBD London cinematic outputs.

Motion must feel:
- intentional
- disciplined
- emotionally weighted
- premium
- psychologically grounded

---

# MOTION PHILOSOPHY

Movement communicates character.

PBD London subjects move like people who carry weight — not physically, but internally.

Every motion should feel:
- deliberate
- earned
- emotionally loaded
- disciplined

Never energetic for its own sake.

---

# SUBJECT ENERGY

The subject should feel:
- disciplined
- emotionally contained
- focused
- ambitious
- mentally occupied
- resilient
- calm under pressure

Performance remains subtle at all times.

Emotion is communicated through:
- posture
- pacing
- eye direction
- silence
- physical tension
- controlled movement

---

# APPROVED MOVEMENT TYPES

## Walking With Purpose

Execution:
- controlled stride
- forward gaze
- relaxed but weighted shoulders
- deliberate pace — not hurried, not idle

Used for:
- progression symbolism
- city environment sequences
- anonymous ambition

---

## Standing In Silence

Execution:
- stillness that reads as restraint, not vacancy
- controlled breathing visible through body
- gaze off-camera or into distance
- subtle physical tension

Used for:
- emotional pressure scenes
- isolation framing
- psychological weight moments

---

## Adjusting Clothing

Execution:
- natural and unhurried
- communicates care in appearance
- never exaggerated
- subtle self-possession

Used for:
- product focus moments
- character establishment
- luxury wardrobe detail reveals

---

## Slow Turn

Execution:
- gradual, weighted rotation
- controlled head movement
- gaze arrives last
- deliberate and purposeful

Used for:
- reveals
- emotional transition
- visual punctuation

---

## Entering Environments

Execution:
- subject moves through threshold with intention
- no hesitation
- environmental awareness communicated through body language
- unhurried

Used for:
- location establishment
- cinematic transitions
- journey and progression sequences

---

## Isolated Motion Through City Space

Execution:
- subject appears self-contained within environment
- no acknowledgement of surroundings
- movement through crowds or empty streets feels the same
- psychological solitude visible

Used for:
- ambition and focus communication
- atmospheric city content

---

# DISALLOWED MOVEMENTS

Never:
- dancing or rhythmic movement
- exaggerated posing
- smiling toward camera
- influencer-style gestures
- waving or pointing
- unnecessary interaction with camera
- exaggerated action sequences
- group hype behaviour

---

# MOTION SPEED

Preferred pacing:
- slow motion
- restrained real-time movement
- weighted physical presence
- controlled stillness

Movement should feel intentional rather than energetic.

Slow motion should be used to:
- extend emotional weight
- reveal fabric texture and movement
- create cinematic atmosphere
- allow the audience to absorb pressure

---

# WARDROBE IN MOTION

Wardrobe movement should feel:
- natural
- premium
- structured

Fabric should:
- move with the subject, not against them
- reveal texture through motion
- feel weighted and real

Materials that perform well in motion:
- heavyweight cotton (controlled drape)
- washed fabric (natural movement)
- matte nylon (structural rigidity)
- brushed fleece (soft weight)
- technical fabric (clean lines in motion)

---

# PACING RULES

Do not rush physical performance.

Allow:
- silence between movements
- stillness as its own statement
- visual tension through non-movement
- breathing room in physical sequences

The pacing should resemble:
editorial fashion cinema, not social media content.

---

# FAILURE CONDITIONS

Reject subject motion that feels:
- performative
- energetic without purpose
- influencer-driven
- trend-motivated
- emotionally empty
- exaggerated or theatrical
