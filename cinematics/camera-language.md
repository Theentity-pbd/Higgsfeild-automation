# PBD London — Camera Language

## PURPOSE

This file defines the camera behaviour system for all PBD London cinematic outputs.

Camera operation must feel:
- observational rather than performative
- emotionally intelligent
- human-operated
- weighted and deliberate
- premium without being ostentatious

---

# CAMERA PHILOSOPHY

The camera is not a spectator.

It is a psychological tool.

Every movement should communicate:
- internal pressure
- emotional tension
- silent ambition
- disciplined observation

The camera should feel like it understands the subject rather than showing off.

---

# MOVEMENT TYPES

## Slow Push-In

Used for:
- emotional intensity
- moments of internal pressure
- subject isolation
- reflection and stillness

Execution:
- extremely controlled forward movement
- subtle handheld realism
- minimal speed variation
- breath-like rhythm

---

## Side Tracking Shot

Used for:
- walking sequences
- movement through city environments
- progression and journey symbolism

Execution:
- smooth lateral glide
- shallow depth of field
- consistent distance from subject
- environment passing naturally behind

---

## Low Angle Ascension

Used for:
- dominance
- elevation symbolism
- ambition and transformation
- visual power

Execution:
- slow rise from below waist level toward upper body or face
- maintains subject above horizon line
- reinforces psychological weight

---

## Static Tension Frame

Used for:
- emotional stillness
- silence
- intimidation
- visual power

Execution:
- minimal or completely static
- environmental movement occurs naturally around subject
- no unnecessary corrections
- holds longer than feels comfortable

---

## Rear Follow Shot

Used for:
- journey symbolism
- anonymous ambition
- solitude and emotional distance

Execution:
- slow trailing camera behind subject through environment
- maintains consistent rear framing
- subject never turns to camera
- environment closes in from sides

---

## Overhead Isolation

Used for:
- scale contrast
- environmental context
- vulnerability within pressure

Execution:
- slow descending or static overhead framing
- subject small within environment
- negative space dominant

---

# LENS FEEL

Preferred characteristics:
- shallow depth of field
- soft compression
- cinematic focus falloff
- subtle anamorphic feel
- natural motion blur

Visual texture should feel:
expensive, tactile, grounded.

Avoid:
- wide-angle distortion
- consumer lens sharpness
- clinical focus pulls
- artificial bokeh effects

---

# FRAMING PRINCIPLES

Preferred framing:
- medium close-up for emotional weight
- waist-level tracking for movement
- silhouette compositions for atmosphere
- wide environmental isolation for scale
- asymmetric framing for visual tension
- negative space compositions for restraint

Avoid:
- centred influencer framing
- vlog-style compositions
- overexposed facial close-ups
- symmetrical setups used repeatedly

---

# MOVEMENT RULES

Camera movement must feel:
- smooth
- deliberate
- heavy
- emotionally observant

Avoid:
- robotic movement
- rapid transitions
- FPV motion overuse
- unnecessary spinning
- aggressive zooms
- music-video style chaos

---

# PACING

Camera pacing should match subject pacing.

Slow subjects demand slow cameras.

Do not contrast subject stillness with camera urgency.

Allow the frame to breathe.

Hold shots longer than feels natural.

Restraint in movement creates psychological tension.

---

# FAILURE CONDITIONS

Reject camera behaviour that feels:
- hyperactive
- show-off
- technically impressive but emotionally empty
- inconsistent with subject energy
- rushed
