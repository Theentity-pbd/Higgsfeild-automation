# PBD London — Lighting Rules

## PURPOSE

This file defines the lighting philosophy and technical behaviour for all PBD London cinematic outputs.

Lighting must feel:
- atmospheric
- directional
- emotionally motivated
- cinematic
- premium

---

# LIGHTING PHILOSOPHY

Light is not decoration.

It is atmosphere and psychology.

PBD London lighting should communicate:
- pressure
- isolation
- discipline
- transformation
- elevation

Every lighting decision should reinforce the emotional intent of the scene.

---

# PRIMARY LIGHTING CHARACTERISTICS

- moody
- directional
- low-key
- contrast-heavy
- atmospheric
- soft shadow edges
- practically motivated

The source of light must always feel believable within the environment.

---

# APPROVED LIGHT SOURCES

## Environmental

- streetlights
- tunnel lighting
- overhead industrial lighting
- window light
- headlights
- diffused natural daylight (overcast preferred)
- reflections off wet surfaces

## Atmospheric

- selective neon accents (used sparingly)
- fire and ember glow
- TV or monitor light
- candlelight in controlled luxury contexts

---

# DISALLOWED LIGHTING

- flat studio lighting
- oversaturated colour lighting
- harsh beauty lighting
- bright commercial three-point setups
- generic ring light aesthetics
- rainbow or multi-colour lighting

---

# COLOUR TEMPERATURE BEHAVIOUR

Preferred temperature range:
- cool to neutral

Cooler tones communicate:
- isolation
- pressure
- mental clarity
- ambition without warmth

Warmer tones used selectively for:
- contrast moments
- transformation beats
- intimacy and depth

Avoid sudden temperature shifts that feel unintentional.

---

# COLOUR PALETTE IN LIGHTING

Primary tones:
- deep black
- charcoal grey
- silver highlights
- off-white diffusion

Accent lighting colours (used rarely and with intent):
- deep red — pressure, tension, emotional weight
- muted amber — warmth, earned comfort, late-night city
- cold blue-white — isolation, urban clarity

Never use:
- oversaturated colour gels
- random neon combinations
- loud gradient lighting
- cheap hype aesthetics

---

# SHADOW BEHAVIOUR

Shadows are part of the composition.

Preferred shadow characteristics:
- soft edges
- directional fall-off
- partial face concealment
- depth-creating negative space
- environmental texture through shadow

Shadow should:
- create mystery
- reinforce emotional control
- add cinematic depth
- frame the subject without overcomplicating

---

# ATMOSPHERIC LIGHTING ELEMENTS

Use atmospheric elements to add depth without spectacle:

- rain mist diffusing streetlight
- fog softening background light
- dust particles catching directional light
- condensation on glass creating texture
- smoke haze softening edges
- reflective wet ground doubling light sources

These should feel:
- natural
- accidental
- cinematic
- never forced

---

# CONTRAST RULES

High contrast is preferred over flat exposure.

Subject should be:
- clearly readable against background
- not perfectly lit
- allowed to fall partially into shadow

Background should:
- recede naturally
- support atmosphere without competing
- remain recognisable but secondary

---

# FAILURE CONDITIONS

Reject lighting that feels:
- flat
- commercial
- artificially colourful
- unmotivated by environment
- technically correct but emotionally empty
