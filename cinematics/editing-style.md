# PBD London — Editing Style

## PURPOSE

This file defines the editorial rhythm, cut behaviour, pacing, and post-production philosophy for all PBD London cinematic outputs.

Editing must feel:
- controlled
- atmospheric
- emotionally intentional
- premium
- disciplined

---

# EDITING PHILOSOPHY

An edit should not be felt.

The audience should be pulled through the film without noticing the cuts.

PBD London editing communicates:
- internal pressure
- elevation through restraint
- disciplined progression
- emotional depth

Editing should serve the atmosphere, not impose on it.

---

# CUT BEHAVIOUR

## Preferred Cut Types

### Hard Cut (used selectively)
- clean transition between shots
- used for tension, contrast, or visual punctuation
- never used rapidly in sequence

### Match Cut
- movement or visual element continues across cut
- creates seamless progression
- reinforces discipline and continuity

### Cut On Movement
- cut triggers on a physical action
- subject motion carries across edit point
- feels natural and weighted

### Slow Dissolve
- used for emotional transitions
- communicates passage of time or internal shift
- should feel earned, not decorative

---

## Disallowed Cut Techniques

- rapid jump cuts
- whip pans as transitions
- music-video style flash cuts
- over-edited sequences
- unnecessary reaction cuts
- zoom transitions
- trendy social media edit styles

---

# PACING

## Pacing Philosophy

Edit to the weight of the emotion, not the tempo of the music.

Slower pacing communicates:
- internal pressure
- discipline
- emotional weight
- luxury and confidence

Faster pacing (used rarely) communicates:
- tension escalation
- transformation
- a single controlled climactic beat

---

## Shot Duration

Preferred shot hold times:
- emotional close-ups: 4–8 seconds minimum
- environmental wides: 5–10 seconds
- action sequences: 3–5 seconds per shot minimum
- static tension frames: hold until discomfort, then release

Avoid:
- cutting before an emotion lands
- transitions that prevent the viewer from settling
- pacing dictated by trend rather than feeling

---

# MUSIC & SOUND RELATIONSHIP

Edit rhythm should feel independent of music tempo.

Music supports atmosphere.
The edit does not chase the beat.

Preferred approach:
- allow shots to breathe across musical phrases
- avoid on-beat cuts unless emotionally motivated
- use silence and ambient sound as editorial tools
- music fades or swells used for emotional transition, not as crutches

---

# COLOUR GRADING DIRECTION

## Grade Philosophy

Colour grading must reinforce the emotional atmosphere.

It is not decoration — it is emotional infrastructure.

---

## Primary Grade Characteristics

- desaturated but not lifeless
- contrast-forward
- shadow detail preserved
- highlight restraint
- skin tones natural and premium
- background recedes into atmosphere

---

## Colour Palette in Grade

Primary tones:
- deep blacks
- cool midtones
- controlled highlights
- muted, realistic skin

Accent colour treatment:
- selective colour lift in shadow (cool blue, deep teal)
- subtle warm push in skin tones only when contrast requires it
- occasional accent colour preservation (deep red, muted amber) when environmentally present

Avoid:
- orange and teal cliché
- flat grey desaturation
- over-processed looks
- trend-driven colour styles
- blown highlights
- crushed shadows with no detail

---

# TITLE & GRAPHIC TREATMENT

If text appears on screen:

- typography must match brand system (see `Visual language md`)
- placement should feel editorial and intentional
- motion should be minimal — slow fade or still placement preferred
- never animated with trendy kinetic effects
- no lower thirds in a broadcast sense
- text is atmospheric, not informational

---

# SOUND DESIGN

Sound design should reinforce cinematic atmosphere.

Preferred elements:
- ambient city environment
- rain, wind, distant traffic
- subtle industrial texture
- fabric movement and footstep detail
- controlled low-frequency presence

Avoid:
- stock sound effects
- over-designed sound beds
- aggressive impact sounds
- cheap audio transitions

---

# FAILURE CONDITIONS

Reject editing that:
- feels rushed
- draws attention to itself
- follows social media trends
- sacrifices emotional weight for pace
- uses transitions as decoration
- over-polishes the humanity out of the footage
